Introduction

In my report on human behaviour regarding sleep patterns, I designed a form adhering to the four guidelines for design forms. One guideline I adhered to was Guideline 3: Consider your respondents. All questions were gender-neutral and phrased in a non-confrontational tone to ensure inclusivity. Additionally, anonymity was maintained, especially in optional questions regarding respondents’ university stage year. This approach prioritised the comfort and privacy of participants, fostering an environment that was facilitative to honest responses. By implementing these considerations, I aimed to gather reliable data while respecting the diverse backgrounds and preferences of the respondents.

The data obtained from the form provides a valuable source for analysing changes in sleep behaviour over time. For example, Question 4 seeks information on the average nightly sleep duration, which provides a numerical measure for tracking changes in sleep patterns over time. Furthermore, Question 7 looks into the factors influencing sleep, providing an understanding of potential external influences that could affect sleep quality and quantity. Correlating responses to these questions allows for a sense of patterns and trends and the factors contributing to changes in sleep behaviour from week to week.

In summary, I made the form user-friendly, respectful, and efficient to collect reliable data on sleep patterns by considering the responders’ needs and preferences. Following Guideline 3 improves the data’s validity and dependability and simplifies the respondents’ experience.

Analytics

I observed that students who work 8 hours or more per week tend to rate their sleep as poor to fair, except those working 20 hours, who rated their sleep as excellent.

I noticed that students without reported sleep troubles tend to sleep more hours per night than those who report sleep issues, indicating a potential correlation between reported sleep troubles and shorter sleep duration.

I observed that most students answered stress as affecting their ability to sleep, suggesting that stress is a prevalent issue impacting students’ sleep quality.

  geom_bar(aes(x = work_hours, fill = sleep_rating)) +
